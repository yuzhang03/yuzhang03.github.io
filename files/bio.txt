Yu Zhang is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Boise State University. He earned his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Kentucky in 2023. He brings extensive industry experience to his academic role. Prior to joining Boise State, he worked as a Machine Learning Engineer at Toyota Automated Logistics, where he developed machine learning solutions for complex problems in computer vision and robotics. His research centers on building efficient, domain-adaptive computer vision systems by developing state-of-the-art models for a wide range of vision and robotics tasks. He also integrates AI with interdisciplinary sciences, including medical imaging, remote sensing, ecology, and astrophysics. He serves as a reviewer for a variety of prestigious conferences and journals, including CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, TPAMI, and TCSVT. He teaches graduate and undergraduate courses in data science, machine learning, and computer vision.
